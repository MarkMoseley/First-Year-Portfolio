PPL Corporation

I interned as a business analyst in the information services department, working mostly to improve the current business processes they already had in place. One of my bigger projects was to streamline the reporting processes for the project managers in my division. The system currently in place was a homemade one using Microsoft excel. My supervisor had to go through line by line to make sure the project was on track which was time consuming and was prone to human error. After meeting with the project managers and my supervisor to develop requirements, I went to work to make a standard reporting format so that all the project managers’ reports would be identical and easy to read. I then set up a series of macros and conditional formatting to immediately highlight any indication that the project was not on track. I did this using sentinel cells in the spreadsheet which looked out for certain issues. For example, if the project was listed as 25% complete but had already used 75% of its budget, then the budget-timeline sentinel would become highlighted in red and my supervisor would then know where to look for more information. As I worked on this project, I sent weekly updates to my supervisor to receive feedback on what head liked, didn't like, and what needed changing. I completed similar projects for a few other departments in the company as well as a few miscellaneous smaller tasks. While there, I was exposed to the Agile development cycle and all of the processes to go into it and its advantages and disadvantages compared to the waterfall development cycle.
